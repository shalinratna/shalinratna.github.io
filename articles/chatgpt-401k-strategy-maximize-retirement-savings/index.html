Common Mistakes and Pro Tips

To get the most out of your 401k planning, avoid these common mistakes:

  • Not contributing enough: Aim to contribute at least 10% of your income towards your 401k.
  • Investing in high-risk options: Stick to low-to-moderate risk investments until you're more comfortable with market volatility.
  • Failing to review and adjust: Regularly review your 401k plan and adjust as needed to ensure it remains aligned with your goals.

Here are some pro tips to keep in mind:

  • Start early: The sooner you begin contributing to your 401k, the more time your money has to grow.
  • Take advantage of employer matching: Contribute enough to maximize your employer's matching contributions.
  • Consider catch-up contributions: If you're 50 or older, take advantage of catch-up contributions to boost your savings.
